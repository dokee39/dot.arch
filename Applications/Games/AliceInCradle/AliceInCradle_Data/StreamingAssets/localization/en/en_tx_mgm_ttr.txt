/* ___ TTR_rule_0 ___ */
<font color="ff:#4fffec">4-ASCEND</font> is a 2-player
competitive board game based on Gomoku.

/* ___ TTR_rule_0_1 ___ */
Local, CPU, and online matches
are supported.

/* ___ TTR_rule_1 ___ */
Players alternate placing stones on the board for their team,
<font color="ff:#5C98FF">Elves</font> (White), and <font color="ff:#FF6483">Monsters</font> (Black).
The objective is to line up stones to <font color="ff:#4fffec">send</font>
attacks to defeat the opponent.

/* ___ TTR_rule_2 ___ */
Players take turns placing stones on
empty spaces of intersecting lines.

/* ___ TTR_rule_3 ___ */
When four stones of the same color are
lined up in any direction, they move to attack.
This is called an <font color="ff:#4fffec">Ascend</font>.

/* ___ TTR_rule_4 ___ */
Each stone used in an <font color="ff:#4fffec">Ascend</font>
represents one point of damage.
Stones used in an <font color="ff:#4fffec">Ascend</font> are
removed from the board.

/* ___ TTR_rule_5 ___ */
After a player makes an <font color="ff:#4fffec">Ascend</font>, the
opponent has a chance to counter with their own.
Stones used by each player will cancel out until one or
both sides remain with none. Leftover stones will
then damage the opposing player.

/* ___ TTR_rule_6 ___ */
The game ends when one player's health reaches 0,
or all spaces on the board are filled.
The winner is the one with the most health,
or the most stones on the board.

/* ___ TTR_rule_7 ___ */
A sound will play when you line
up three stones in a row (Riichi).
Always have a Riichi prepared in
case your opponent <font color="ff:#4fffec">Ascends</font>.
Otherwise you are guaranteed to take damage.

/* ___ TTR_rule_8 ___ */
As the game progresses, <font color="ff:#DFFFB4">Mana Plant</font>s
will sprout up on the board.
Stones placed on a <font color="ff:#DFFFB4">Mana Plant</font> during
an <font color="ff:#4fffec">Ascend</font> will add an additional point
when taking out opposing stones, but will not
inflict additional damage to HP.

/* ___ TTR_rule_9 ___ */
After your opponent has made an <font color="ff:#4fffec">Ascend</font>,
you can place one of your stones where their's
used to be and perform a <font color="ff:#FFB5FF">Back Attack</font> to destroy it.
If there was a <font color="ff:#DFFFB4">Mana Plant</font> on that space, you'll gain
control of it, instantly netting two points of advantage.

/* ___ TTR_rule_10 ___ */
You can still make a <font color="ff:#FFB5FF">Back Attack</font>
without creating an <font color="ff:#4fffec">Ascend</font>.
Saving one extra point of damage
may determine the winner.


/* ___ TTR_rule_11 ___ */
Mana Plants that grow immediately after an <font color="ff:#4fffec">Ascend</font>
tend to grow in spaces that favor the attacker.
But, if you <font color="ff:#4fffec">Ascend</font> continuously, they'll
thin out and grow elsewhere.
When things are at a standstill, are you willing
to concede a few points of damage to win the war?


/* ___ TTR_rule_12 ___ */
Online matches against random players and friends are available.
We use a free server to host these, apologies for any connection issues.
If you want to play against a friend, share your room ID with them via Discord or other means.

/* ___ TTR_rule_13 ___ */
When a random match is over, player rank changes by how many
games the player has won in the last 10 matches divided by 2 (max +4).
This number is simply an indication of how well the player
is doing, and does not affect matchmaking in any way.
Winning against a higher rank opponent does not confer extra benefits.
